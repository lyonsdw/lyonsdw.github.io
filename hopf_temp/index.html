The Hopf Fibration

The Hopf fibration, named after Heinz Hopf who studied it in a 1931 paper, has become an important object in mathematics and physics. Unfortunately, it is little known in the undergraduate curriculum. The thesis of this web tutorial is that the Hopf fibration can be accessible to undergraduate mathematics and physics majors. We have structured our presentation to facilitate individualized study.
