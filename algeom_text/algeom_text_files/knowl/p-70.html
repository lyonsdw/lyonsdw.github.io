Let \(k\) be a field, such as the rational numbers \(\Q\text{,}\) the real numbers \(\R\text{,}\) or the complex numbers \(\C\text{.}\) We write \(k^\ast\) to denote the set of nonzero elements in \(k\text{.}\)

Definition 1.1.12. Group of nonzero elements in a field.
Let \(k\) be a field. The set \(k^\ast\text{,}\) together with the binary operation of multiplication, is called the group of nonzero elements in the field \(k\text{.}\)
