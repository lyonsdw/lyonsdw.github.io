Exercise 6. Cyclic groups, reconciling two uses of the term "generator".
  1. Show that if a group \(G\) is cyclic with generator \(g\text{,}\) then \(G=\langle g\rangle\text{.}\)
  2. Show that if a finite group \(G\) is cyclic with generator \(g\text{,}\) then
    \begin{equation*} G=\langle g \rangle = \{g^0,g^1,g^2,\ldots,g^{|G|-1}\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
