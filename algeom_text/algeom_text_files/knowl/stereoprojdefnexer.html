Checkpoint 0.2.5.

Find \(s^{-1}(3+i)\text{.}\) It is intuitively clear that stereographic projection is a bijection. Make this rigorous by finding a formula for the inverse \(s^{-1}\colon \C^+\to S^2\) and checking that \(s\circ s^{-1}\) is the identity of \(\C^+\) and that \(s^{-1}\circ s\) is the identity on \(S^2\text{.}\)
