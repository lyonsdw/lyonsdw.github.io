Section 0.1 Complex Numbers

The set of complex numbers, denoted \(\C\text{,}\) is in one-to-one correspondence with the 2-dimensional real plane \(\R^2\text{.}\) We will write \(z\leftrightarrow (x,y)\) to denote that the complex number \(z\) corresponds to the ordered pair \((x,y)\) of real numbers.

Subsection 0.1.1 Real and imaginary parts

Given a complex number \(z\) corresponding to the point \((x,y)\) in \(\R^2\text{,}\) we say that \(x\) is the real part of \(z\) and that \(y\) is the imaginary part of \(z\text{,}\) denoted \(\re(z)=x\) and \(\im(z)=y\text{.}\) The complex numbers contain the real numbers \(\R\) as a subset. The real number \(x\text{,}\) which is also the complex number \(x\text{,}\) corresponds to the ordered pair \((x,0)\text{,}\) The complex number \(i\) corresponds to the ordered pair \((0,1)\text{.}\) Here is a summary so far.

\begin{align*} z \amp \leftrightarrow (\re(z),\im(z))\\ x\in \R \amp \leftrightarrow (x,0)\\ i \amp \leftrightarrow (0,1) \end{align*}

Subsection 0.1.2 Modulus and argument

Given a complex number \(z\leftrightarrow (x,y)\text{,}\) let \((r,\theta)\) be polar coordinates for the point \((x,y)\) such that \(r\geq 0\) and \(\theta\) is measured in radians. The modulus or norm of \(z\text{,}\) denoted \(|z|\text{,}\) is defined to be the polar coordinate \(r=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}\) and the argument of \(z\text{,}\) denoted \(\arg{z}\text{,}\) is the polar coordinate \(\theta\text{,}\) that is, the oriented angle made by the real vector \((x,y)\) with the positive real axis. In other words, \((|z|,\arg{z})\) are polar coordinates for the point \((x,y)\text{.}\) Here is a summary.

\begin{equation} z\leftrightarrow (x,y) = \left(|z|\cos( \arg{z}),|z|\sin(\arg{z})\right) \label{normargdef}\tag{0.1.1} \end{equation}

Subsection 0.1.3 Addition and multiplication of complex numbers

Given complex numbers \(z\leftrightarrow (x,y)\) and \(z'\leftrightarrow (x',y')\text{,}\) the sum \(z+z'\) is defined by

\begin{equation} z+z' \leftrightarrow (x+x',y+y').\label{complexadddef}\tag{0.1.2} \end{equation}

In other words, complex addition corresponds to real vector addition. See Figure Figure 0.1.1.

The product \(zz'\) is defined by

\begin{align} |zz'| \amp = |z||z'|\label{complexmultdefnorm}\tag{0.1.3}\\ \arg(zz') \amp = \arg{z}+\arg{z'}\label{complexmultdefarg}\tag{0.1.4} \end{align}

The interaction between complex addition and multiplication is expressed by the distributive law, which says that the following holds for any complex \(z,u,v\text{.}\) See Figure Figure 0.1.2.

\begin{equation} z(u+v) = zu+zv \label{distributivelaw}\tag{0.1.5} \end{equation}

Below are a number of relationships arising from the definitions of complex addition and multiplication. Let \(a,b,c,d\) be real numbers and let \(z,u,v\) be complex numbers. The following relationships hold.  1 Equations (0.1.6), (0.1.7), and (0.1.9) say that complex addition, multiplication, and norm extend the corresponding operations on the reals. Equation (0.1.10) is expressed by saying 1 is the multiplicative identity.

\begin{align} a+ib \amp \leftrightarrow (a,b)\label{addextends}\tag{0.1.6}\\ a+b \;\;\text{(complex sum)} \amp= a+b \;\;\text{(real sum)}\label{multextends}\tag{0.1.7}\\ ab \;\;\text{(complex product)} \amp = ab \;\;\text{(real product)}\tag{0.1.8}\\ |a| \;\;\text{(complex norm)} \amp = |a| \;\;\text{(real absolute value)}\label{normextends}\tag{0.1.9}\\ 1z \amp = z1 = z \label{multidentity}\tag{0.1.10}\\ i^2 \amp = -1\label{i2isminus1}\tag{0.1.11}\\ (a+ib)+ (c+id) \amp = (a+c) + i(b+d)\tag{0.1.12}\\ (a+ib)(c+id) \amp = (ac-bd) + i(ad+bc)\label{complexmultrectangular}\tag{0.1.13} \end{align}
