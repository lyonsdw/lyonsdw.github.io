Below are a number of relationships arising from the definitions of complex addition and multiplication. Let \(a,b,c,d\) be real numbers and let \(z,u,v\) be complex numbers. The following relationships hold.  1 

\begin{align} a+ib \amp \leftrightarrow (a,b)\tag{0.1.6}\\ a+b \;\;\text{(complex sum)} \amp= a+b \;\;\text{(real sum)}\tag{0.1.7}\\ ab \;\;\text{(complex product)} \amp = ab \;\;\text{(real product)}\tag{0.1.8}\\ |a| \;\;\text{(complex norm)} \amp = |a| \;\;\text{(real absolute value)}\tag{0.1.9}\\ 1z \amp = z1 = z \tag{0.1.10}\\ i^2 \amp = -1\tag{0.1.11}\\ (a+ib)+ (c+id) \amp = (a+c) + i(b+d)\tag{0.1.12}\\ (a+ib)(c+id) \amp = (ac-bd) + i(ad+bc)\tag{0.1.13} \end{align}
