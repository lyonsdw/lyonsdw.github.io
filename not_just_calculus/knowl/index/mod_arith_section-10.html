Remark on the proof of PropositionÂ 1.2.5. The proof uses a logic structure called proof by contradiction. To show that a claim is true ("there are infinitely many primes"), a proof by contradiction begins by supposing that the opposite of the claim is true ("suppose there are only finitely many primes"). Next, we show that there is an impossible consequence ("the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic is false"). We conclude that the opposite claim ("there are only finitely many primes") must be false, so the original claim ("there are infinitely many primes") is true.
