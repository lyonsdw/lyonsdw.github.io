Here is how complex multiplication looks in polar form. For \(z=r\expi(\theta), w=s\expi(\phi)\text{,}\) we have From this it is easy to see that for \(r\neq 0\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation} \left(r\expi(\theta)\right)\; \left(\frac{1}{r}\expi(-\theta)\right) = 1.\tag{2.3.17} \end{equation}
For \(z=r\expi(\theta)\) with \(r\neq 0\text{,}\) we call \(\frac{1}{r}\expi(-\theta)\) the multiplicative inverse of \(z\text{,}\) denoted \({1}/{z}\) or \(z^{-1}\text{.}\) We define the quotient \(z/w\) or \(\frac{z}{w}\) by
\begin{equation} \frac{z}{w} = zw^{-1}.\tag{2.3.18} \end{equation}
