Remark on the statement and proof of PropositionÂ 1.2.6. The phrase "if and only if" means that the following two statements hold.
  1. If \(a\equiv b \pmod{m}\text{,}\) then \(a\MOD m = b \MOD m\text{.}\)
  2. If \(a\MOD m = b \MOD m\text{,}\) then \(a\equiv b \pmod{m}\text{.}\)
It is often a good idea to construct a proof for an "if and only if" statement in two parts, one part for each of the two "if-then" statements. Statement 1 is called the "only if" part of the "if and only if" statement, and statement 2 is called the "if" part of the if and only if statement.
