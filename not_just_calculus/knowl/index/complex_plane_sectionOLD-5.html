Let \(z \leftrightarrow (a,b)=(r\cos \theta,r\sin\theta)\) and \(w\leftrightarrow (c,d)=(s\cos\phi,s\sin\phi)\) be complex numbers with \(r,s\geq 0\text{.}\) The sum of \(z\) and \(w\text{,}\) denoted \(z+w\text{,}\) is defined by
\begin{equation} z+w \leftrightarrow (a+c,b+d).\tag{2.3.1} \end{equation}
The product of \(z\) and \(w\text{,}\) denoted \(zw\text{,}\) is defined by
\begin{equation} zw \leftrightarrow rs(\cos(\theta+\phi),\sin(\theta+\phi)).\tag{2.3.2} \end{equation}
If \(\alpha\) is real and \(z\leftrightarrow P\text{,}\) then (2.3.2) takes the following form.
\begin{equation} \alpha z \leftrightarrow \alpha P\tag{2.3.3} \end{equation}
