In mathematics, a statement is a sentence that is either true or false. For example, the sentence "\(3|6\)" is a true statement (because \(6=3\cdot 2\)), the sentence "\(6|3\)" is a false statement (because there is no integer \(k\) such that \(3=6k\)) and the expression "\(6/3\)" is not a statement at all. It is just a representation of the number 2 (6 divided by 3), and it is neither true nore false.
