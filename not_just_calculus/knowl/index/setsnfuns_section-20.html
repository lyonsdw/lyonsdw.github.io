A consequence of Proposition 1.1.3, Proposition 1.1.4, and Proposition 1.1.5 is that the following conditions on a function \(f\colon S\to T\) are equivalent.
  1. The function \(f\) is bijective.
  2. There exists a function \(g\colon T\to S\) such that \(g\of f = \One_S\) and \(f\of g = \One_T\text{.}\)
The statement that these conditions are equivalent means that if \(f\) satisfies condition 1, then \(f\) satisfies condition 2, and vice-versa. If \(f\) satisfies these conditions, then \(f\) is called invertible, with inverse function \(f^{-1}\text{.}\) We record these observations as a corollary.
