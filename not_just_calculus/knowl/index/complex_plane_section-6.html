Given complex numbers \(z \leftrightarrow (a,b)=(r\cos \theta,r\sin\theta)\) and \(w\leftrightarrow (c,d)=(s\cos\phi,s\sin\phi)\text{,}\) the sum of \(z\) and \(w\text{,}\) denoted \(z+w\text{,}\) is defined to be the complex number that corresponds to the Cartesian sum \((a,b)+(c,d)=(a+c,b+d)\text{.}\)
\begin{equation} z+w \leftrightarrow (a+c,b+d)\tag{2.3.1} \end{equation}
and the product of \(z\) and \(w\text{,}\) denoted \(zw\text{,}\) is defined by
\begin{equation} zw \leftrightarrow rs(\cos(\theta+\phi),\sin(\theta+\phi)).\tag{2.3.2} \end{equation}
