Given a complex number \(z\) corresponding to the point \((x,y)=(r\cos \theta,r\sin \theta)\) with \(r\geq 0\text{,}\) we say that \(x\) is the real part of \(z\text{,}\) and that \(y\) is the imaginary part of \(z\text{,}\) denoted \(\re(z)=x\text{,}\) and \(\im(z)=y\text{,}\) respectively. We say that \(r\) is the norm or modulus of \(z\text{,}\) and we say that \(\theta\) is an argument of \(z\text{,}\) denoted \(|z|=r\) and \(\arg(z)=\theta\), respectively.
