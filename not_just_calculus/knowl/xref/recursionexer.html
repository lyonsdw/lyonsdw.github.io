Exercise 3. Recursion.

The sequence \(L(n)\) for \(n=1,2,3,\ldots\) satisfies the recursive relation \(L(n+1)=L(n)+m\text{.}\) (A recursive relation says how the next entry in a sequence is determined by one or more previous entries.) The sequence \(E(n)\) for \(n=1,2,3,\ldots\) satisfies the recursive relation \(E(n+1)=rE(n)\text{.}\) Suppose that there is a function \(M(t)\) that satisfies the recursive relation \(M(n+1)=rM(n)+m\) for \(n=1,2,3,\ldots\text{.}\) Find a formula for \(M(t)\) in terms of \(M_0=M(0),r,m\text{.}\)
