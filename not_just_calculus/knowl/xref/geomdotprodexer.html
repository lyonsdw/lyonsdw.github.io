Exercise 7. Geometric content of the dot product.

Let \({\mathbf x},{\mathbf y}\) be vectors in \(\R^2\text{.}\) Let \(O=(0,0)\) denote the origin, let \(P=O+{\mathbf x}\text{,}\) and let \(Q=O+{\mathbf y}\) Show that
\begin{equation} {\mathbf x} \cdot {\mathbf y} = \left\Vert{\mathbf x}\right\Vert \left\Vert{\mathbf y}\right\Vert \cos \theta\tag{2.1.11} \end{equation}
where \(\theta\) is the measure of the angle \(\angle POQ\text{.}\) Hint: Start by writing \(P,Q\) in polar coordinates, then use previous exercises.
