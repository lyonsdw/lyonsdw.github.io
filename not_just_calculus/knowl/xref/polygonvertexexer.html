Exercise 8.

Let \(n\) be a positive integer. For \(k\) in the range \(0\leq k\leq n-1\text{,}\) let \(P_k=\wrap\left(\frac{2\pi k}{n}\right)\text{,}\) where \(\wrap\) denotes the wrapping function.
  1. Show that the points \(P_0,P_1,\ldots,P_{n-1}\) are vertices of a regular \(n\)-gon inscribed in the unit circle.
  2. Show that the following holds for \(0\leq k,\ell\leq n-1\text{.}\)
    \begin{equation*} P_k\odot P_\ell = P_{k+_n \ell} \end{equation*}
Answer.
  1. (just notice that the radian angle \(2\pi/n\) is \(1/n\)th of a full revolution)
  2. Use the division algorithm to write \(k+\ell = nq +r\) for some integers \(q,r\) with \(r=(k+\ell) \MOD m\text{.}\) We have
    \begin{equation*} P_k\odot P_\ell = W\left((k+\ell)\frac{2\pi}{n}\right) = W\left(2\pi q + r\frac{2\pi}{n}\right)= W\left(r\frac{2\pi}{n}\right)=P_{k+_n \ell}. \end{equation*}
