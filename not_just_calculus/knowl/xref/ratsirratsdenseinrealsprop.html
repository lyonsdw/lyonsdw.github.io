Proposition 1.8.5.

The set \(\Q\) of rational numbers is dense in \(\R\text{.}\) The set \(\R\setminus \Q\) of irrational numbers is dense in \(\R\text{.}\)

Proof.

\(\Q\)\(\R\text{.}\)\(a,b\)\(a\lt b\text{,}\)\(\epsilon=b-a\text{.}\)\(n\)\(1/n \lt \epsilon\text{.}\)\(1/n\)\(a\)\(b\text{,}\)\(m\)\(a\lt m/n \lt b\text{.}\)\(k/n,(k+1)/n\)\(1/n\)
\begin{equation*} k/n \leq a \lt b\leq (k+1)/n. \end{equation*}
\(b-a\leq (k+1)/n -k/n = 1/n\text{,}\)\((a,b)\)\(\Q\)\(\R\text{.}\)
