Proposition 1.1.7.

Let \(f=(S,T,G)\) be a bijective function, then the following hold.
  1. Every \(t\) in \(T\) is the right entry in exactly one element of \(G\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(G'=\{(t,s)\colon (s,t)\in G\}\text{.}\) Then \((T,S,G')\) is a function.
  3. The function \(g=(T,S,G')\) has the property that \(g\of f=\One_S\) and \(f\of g=\One_T\text{.}\)
