Course Goals

David W. Lyons
Fall 2014

revised 4 November 2014
The primary goal of the course is intellectual growth through
  • understanding course material,
  • critical reading,
  • analytical thinking, and
  • clear, concise communication (oral, written, or both).

Achievement of course goals requires the following of the individual student.

  • desire for learning
  • willingness to work hard
  • time
  • persistence
Applying these to the best of one's ability leads to a joyful and rewarding learning process. The lack of one or more obstructs fulfillment.

Learning objectives are "skills, competencies, and abilities students demonstrate in the course, and what knowledge students acquire in the course" (definition taken from Elements of Course Syllabi from the Office of the Associate Dean of Academic Affairs). Students who desire to learn, work hard, spend at least two hours on homework outside of class for each hour in class, and persist until they master each topic, will:

  • demonstrate ability to construct meaning and understanding by reading technical material;
  • demonstrate skill in analytical thinking and problem solving;
  • demonstrate skill in clear, concise communication (written, oral, or both);
  • and demonstrate mastery of the course material at a satisfactory (grade `C') level or higher.

Assessment is the measurement of progress toward course goals and student learning objectives, followed by the use of such measurement to realize these goals and objectives more effectively. Some forms of assessment used in this course are

  • scores on course assignments,
  • instructor comments (oral, written, or both),
  • rubric-based analysis of student work, and
  • a student opinion survey (IDEA)
