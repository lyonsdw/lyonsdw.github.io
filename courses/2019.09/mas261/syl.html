Course Description

We extend the theory and applications of differentiation and integration, developed in introductory calculus courses, to functions involving more variables.

Prerequisites

MAS 112 or MAS 162, or permission by the instructor

Texts and Materials

  • Schlicker et al., Active Calculus--Multivariable, 2018 edition
  • Lyons, Linear Algebra Basics Supplementary Notes

Reading and Problem List

  • Part 1. Introduction to multivariable functions
    • Chapter 1
  • Part 2. Introduction to linear algebra
    • Supplementary Notes, Sections 2.1--2.4
  • Part 3. Derivatives of multivariable functions
    • Chapter 2
    • Supplementary Notes, Section 2.5
  • Part 4. Integrals of multivariable functions
    • Chapter 3
Course reading assignments are the text sections listed above. The amount of problem solving practice needed to achieve mastery varies by individual. Here are two extremes, for problems in the textbook.
  • Maximum practice needed: work every "Example", work all of the "Activity" problems, and work all of the "Exercises" in each section
  • Minimum practice needed: work approximately every other problem in the "Exercises" at the end of each section
You should work all of the exercises in the Supplementary Notes.
