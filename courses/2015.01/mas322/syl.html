Course Description

This course is an introduction to the theory of one of the most fundamental abstract algebraic structures called the group. Algebraic structures---in particular, structures arising from symmetries---pervade every area of mathematics and its applications. Groups provide language and technique for analyzing symmetries in diverse settings.

Prerequisites

Reading and Problem List

The reading material for the course is Chapters 1 through 11 in the text book, plus the Supplementary Notes. The problem assignments are every other odd numbered item in the Exercises at the end of each chapter in the text book, plus all the exercises in the Supplementary Notes.
