Course Goals

David W. Lyons
Spring 2013

revised 19 Dec 2012
The primary goal of the course is intellectual growth through
  • understanding course material,
  • critical reading,
  • analytical thinking, and
  • clear, concise writing.
Here are links to the course goals documents for General Education and the Department of Mathematical Sciences.
  • General Education Goals
  • Department of Mathematical Sciences Goals

Achievement of course goals requires the following of the individual student.

  • desire for learning
  • willingness to work hard
  • time
  • persistence
Applying these to the best of one's ability leads to a joyful and rewarding learning process. The lack of one or more obstructs fulfillment.

Assessment is the measurement of progress toward course goals, followed by the use of such measurement to realize course goals more effectively. Some forms of assessment used in this course are

  • scores on course assignments,
  • instructor comments on written work,
  • rubric-based analysis of student work, and
  • a student opinion survey.
