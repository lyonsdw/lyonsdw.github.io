Let \(L\colon \R^n\to \R^m\) be a linear transformation and let \(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}\) be vectors in $\R^n$. Suppose that the two linearity properties (i) and (ii) hold, and let \(a,b\) be scalars. We have

\begin{align*} L(a\mathbf{x}+b\mathbf{y}) \amp = L(a\mathbf{x}) + L(b\mathbf{y}) \amp \text{(by linearity property (i))}\\ \amp = aL\mathbf{x}+bL\mathbf{y} \amp \text{(by linearity property (ii))}. \end{align*}

Conversely, suppose that (2.8) holds. Choosing \(a=b=1\text{,}\) we have (i)

\begin{equation*} L(\mathbf{x}+\mathbf{y}) = L(a\mathbf{x}+b\mathbf{y})= aL\mathbf{x} + bL\mathbf{y} = L\mathbf{x} + L\mathbf{y}. \end{equation*}

Choosing \(b=0\text{,}\) we have (ii)

\begin{equation*} L(a\mathbf{x})=L(a\mathbf{x}+b\mathbf{y})=aL\mathbf{x}+bL\mathbf{y}=aL\mathbf{x}. \end{equation*}
