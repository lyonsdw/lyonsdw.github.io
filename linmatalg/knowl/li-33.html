We have \(\mathbf{c}_j=L\mathbf{e}_j\) (by the definition of \([L]\)), so the first equality is (2.4). For the second equality, compare \(i\)th entries of the vectors on the left and the right sides. The \(i\)th entry on both sides is
\begin{equation*} a_{i1}x_1 + a_{i2}x_2 + \cdots + a_{in}x_n. \end{equation*}
