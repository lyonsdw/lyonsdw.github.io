Instructor’s solution for Exercise 2.4.6.

Let \(L\colon \R^n\to \R^m\) and let \(M\colon R^p\to \R^n\) be linear transformations. First we check linearity property (i) for the composition \(LM\text{.}\) Let \(\mathbf{t},\mathbf{s}\) be elements of \(\R^p\) and let \(\alpha\) be a real number. We have
\begin{align*} LM(\mathbf{t}+\mathbf{s}) \amp = L(M(\mathbf{t}+\mathbf{s})) \amp \text{(definition)}\\ \amp = L(M\mathbf{t}+M\mathbf{s})\amp \text{(linearity (i))}\\ \amp = L(M\mathbf{t})+L(M\mathbf{s})\amp \text{(linearity (i))}\\ \amp = LM\mathbf{t} + LM\mathbf{s}\amp \text{(definition)}. \end{align*}
For property (ii), we have
\begin{align*} LM(\alpha\mathbf{t}) \amp = L(M\alpha\mathbf{t}) \amp \text{(definition)}\\ \amp = L(\alpha M\mathbf{t}) \amp \text{(linearity (ii))}\\ \amp = \alpha L(M\mathbf{t}) \amp \text{(linearity (ii))}\\ \amp = \alpha LM\mathbf{t}\amp \text{(definition)}. \end{align*}
