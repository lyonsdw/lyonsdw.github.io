Exercise 5.

The length or norm of vector \(\mathbf{x}=(x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_n)\text{,}\) denoted \(|\mathbf{x}|\) (or often \(\|\mathbf{x}\|\)), is defined by

\begin{equation*} |\mathbf{x}|^2 = \mathbf{x}\cdot \mathbf{x} = x_1^2 + x_2^2 + \cdots + x_n^2. \end{equation*}

A vector \(\mathbf{x}\) is said be be normalized or have norm 1 if \(|\mathbf{x}|=1\text{.}\)

  1. Show that, for any vector \(\mathbf{x}\) and any \(\alpha>0\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation*} |\alpha\mathbf{x}|=\alpha|\mathbf{x}|. \end{equation*}
  2. Show that, for any nonzero vector \(\mathbf{x}\text{,}\) the vector \(\frac{\mathbf{x}}{|\mathbf{x}|}\) has norm 1.
