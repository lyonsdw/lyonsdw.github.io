Exercise 5.

The dot product has the following properties that look like the properties in the definition of linear map.

\begin{align} \mathbf{u}\cdot (\alpha \mathbf{v}) \amp = \alpha \mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{v}\tag{2.8}\\ \mathbf{u}\cdot (\mathbf{v}+\mathbf{w}) \amp = \mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{v} + \mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{w}\tag{2.9} \end{align}

for all $\mathbf{u},\mathbf{v},\mathbf{w}$ in $\R^n$ and scalars $\alpha$. Show that these properties hold.
