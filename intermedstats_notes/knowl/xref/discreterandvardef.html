Definition 4.1.

A function \(X\colon\Omega \to Y\text{,}\) where \((\Omega,P)\) is a discrete probability model (finite or countably infinite) and \(Y\) is a set, is called a discrete random variable. If \(Y\) is a subset of the real numbers, then \(X\) is called a quantitative random variable; otherwise, \(X\) is called qualitative.
