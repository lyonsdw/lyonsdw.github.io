Exercise 4. The triangle inequality for the hyperbolic metric.

  1. Show that the triangle inequality holds with strict equality when \(a,b,c\) are collinear and \(c\) is between \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\) Hint
  2. Show that the triangle inequality holds with strict inequality when \(a,b,c\) are collinear and \(c\) is not between \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\)
  3. Let \(p\in \D\text{.}\) Let \(L\) be the line that contains \(p\) and intersects the real line orthogonally at a point \(p'\) (this is possible by ExerciseÂ 3.3.6.3). Show that \(d(0,p)\geq d(0,p')\text{,}\) with equality if and only if \(p\) is real. Hint
  4. Given arbitrary \(a,b,c\text{,}\) apply a transformation \(T\) to send \(a\to 0\) and \(b\) to a nonnegative real point. Drop a perpendicular from \(Tc\) to the real line, say, to \(c'\text{.}\) Apply results from the previous steps of this outline.
