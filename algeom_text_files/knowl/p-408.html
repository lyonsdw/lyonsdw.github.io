First consider the case where no three of \(A,A',P,R\) are collinear. Applying the Fundamental Theorem of Projective Geometry, we may assume \(A=[e_1]\text{,}\) \(A'=[e_2]\text{,}\) \(P=[e_3]\text{,}\) and \(R=[e_0]\text{.}\)

  • Check that \(AR=[0,-1,1]\) and \(A'R=[-1,0,1]\text{.}\)
  • Explain why it follows that \(B'=[r,1,1]\) and \(B=[1,s,1]\) for some \(r,s\text{.}\)
  • Explain why \(C=[rs,s,1]\) and \(C'=[r,rs,1]\text{.}\)
  • Explain why \(Q=[rs,rs,1]\text{.}\)
  • Observe that \(P,Q,R\) all lie on \([1,-1,0]\text{.}\)
  • Finally, address the degenerate case(s) where \(A,A',P,R\) are not in general position.
