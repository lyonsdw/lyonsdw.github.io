Just observe that the order of a \(k\)-cycle is \(k\text{.}\) Now if \(\sigma=\alpha_1\cdots\alpha_r\) is a factoring of \(\sigma\) into a product of disjoint cycles \(\alpha_i\text{,}\) we have \(\sigma^s=\alpha_1^s\cdots\alpha_r^s\) so if \(\sigma^s=e\) it must be that \(s\) is a multiple of the length of each of the \(\alpha_i\text{.}\) By the definition of the order of an element, the order of \(\sigma\) must be the least common multiple of the lengths of the \(\alpha_i\text{.}\)
