Instructor's solution for Exercise 3.2.6.6.

  1. Apply Proposition 3.2.16 to a Möbius transformation that takes three points on a cline \(C\) to three other points on \(C\text{.}\)

  2. Let \(\omega = e^{i2\pi/3}\) and let \(Tz=(z,\omega,\omega^2,\omega^3=1)\text{.}\) We have the following.

    \begin{align*} Tz\amp =\frac{z-\omega^2}{z-\omega^3}\frac{\omega-\omega^3}{\omega-\omega^2}\\ \amp = (1+\omega)\left(\frac{z-\omega^2}{z-1}\right)\\ T \amp \leftrightarrow \twotwo{1+\omega}{-\omega^2-1}{1}{-1}\\ T^{-1} \amp \leftrightarrow \twotwo{-1}{\omega^2+1}{-1}{1+\omega} \end{align*}
    We have
    \begin{align*} z^{\ast C} \amp = T^{-1}((Tz)^\ast)\\ \amp = T^{-1} \left(\frac{(1+\omega^2)z^\ast -\omega-1}{z^\ast-1}\right)\\ \amp = \frac{-[(1+\omega^2)z^\ast -\omega-1] +(z-1)(\omega^2+1)}{-[(1+\omega^2)z^\ast -\omega-1] +(1+\omega)(z^\ast-1)}\\ \amp = \cdots = \frac{1}{z^\ast} \end{align*}

  3. Let \(Sz=\frac{z-a}{r}\) so that \(S\) takes \(C\) to the unit circle. Applying Proposition 3.2.16, we have

    \begin{equation*} (Sz)^{\ast S(C)} = \frac{1}{(Sz)^\ast} = \frac{r}{(z-a)^\ast}. \end{equation*}
    Setting this last expression equal to \(S(z^{\ast C})\) and applying \(S^{-1}\) to both sides, we have
    \begin{equation*} z^{\ast C} = \frac{r^2}{(z-a)^\ast} + a \end{equation*}
    as desired.

  4. Let \(C\) make an angle \(t\) with the positive real axis, and let \(a\) be a point on \(C\text{.}\) Let \(Sz=e^{-it}(z-a)\text{,}\) so that \(S(C)=\R\text{.}\) Applying Proposition 3.2.16, we have \((Sz)^{\ast S(C)} = (Sz)^\ast = e^{it}(z-a)^\ast\text{.}\) Setting this last expression equal to \(S(z^{\ast C}\) and applying \(S^{-1}\) to both sides, we have

    \begin{equation*} z^{\ast C} = e^{i2t}(z-a)^\ast + a. \end{equation*}
    We know that \(Sz,(Sz)^\ast\) are reflections of one another across the real axis. Because \(S^{-1}\) is a rigid motion (rotation followed by a translation, so distances and angles are preserved), the pair \(z,z^{\ast C}\) must be reflections of each other across the line \(C\text{.}\)
