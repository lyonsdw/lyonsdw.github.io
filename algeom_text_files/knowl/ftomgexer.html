Exercise 2. Proof of the Fundamental Theorem of Möbius Geometry.

  1. Let \(z_1,z_2,z_3\) be three distinct points in \(\C\text{.}\) Verify that

    \begin{equation} Tz=\frac{z-z_2}{z-z_3}\frac{z_1-z_3}{z_1-z_2}\tag{3.2.11} \end{equation}
    satisfies \(Tz_1=1,Tz_2=0,Tz_3=\infty\text{.}\)

  2. Complete the proof of Lemma 3.2.9 by finding explicit formulas for \(T\) for each of the three cases \(z_1=\infty\text{,}\) \(z_2=\infty\) and \(z_3=\infty\text{.}\)

  3. Complete the proof of Lemma 3.2.10.

  4. Complete the details of the proof of the Fundamental Theorem of Möbius Geometry Proposition 3.2.11. That is, check that \(T\) satisfies \(Tz_i=w_i\) for \(i=1,2,3\text{,}\) and check that \(S\circ T^{-1}\) fixes three points.

Consequence of this exercise: We have the following explicit formulas for the cross ratio.

\begin{align} (z_0,z_1,z_2,z_3) \amp= \frac{z_0-z_2}{z_0-z_3}\frac{z_1-z_3}{z_1-z_2}\tag{3.2.12}\\ (z_0,\infty,z_2,z_3)\amp = \frac{z_0-z_2}{z_0-z_3}\tag{3.2.13}\\ (z_0,z_1,\infty,z_3)\amp = \frac{z_1-z_3}{z_0-z_3}\tag{3.2.14}\\ (z_0,z_1,z_2,\infty)\amp = \frac{z_0-z_2}{z_1-z_2}\tag{3.2.15} \end{align}
