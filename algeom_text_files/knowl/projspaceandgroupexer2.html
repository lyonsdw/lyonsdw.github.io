Exercise 5. The projective linear group action on projective space.

Let \(V\) be a vector space over a field \(\F\) (in this course, the base field \(\F\) is either the real numbers \(\R\) or the complex numbers \(\C\)), and let \(V^\ast := V\setminus\{0\}\) denote the set of nonzero vectors in \(V\text{.}\) The field \(\F\) acts on \(V^\ast\) by scalar multiplication. The set of orbits

\begin{equation*} V^{\ast}/\F \end{equation*}

is called the projectivization of \(V\text{,}\) or simply projective space, and is denoted \(\Proj(V)\) The projective equivalence relation \(\sim\) on the set \(V\setminus \{0\}\) of nonzero elements in \(V\) is defined by \(x\sim y\) if and only if \(x=\alpha y\) for some nonzero \(\alpha\in\F\text{.}\) The set of equivalence classes \(\{(V\setminus \{0\})/\!\!\sim\}\text{,}\) denoted \(\Proj(V)\text{,}\) is called projective space.

  1. Verify that the projective equivalence relation is indeed an equivalence relation.
  2. Verify that the group of \(GL(V)\) (the group of invertible linear transformations of \(V\)) acts on \(\Proj(V)\) by
    \begin{equation} g\cdot [{v}] = [g({v})]\tag{2.5.2} \end{equation}
    for \(g\in GL(V)\) and \({v}\in V\setminus \{0\}\text{.}\)
  3. Show that the kernel of the map \(GL(V)\to \Perm(\Proj(V))\) given by (2.5.1) is the subgroup \(K=\{\alpha\Id\colon \alpha\in \F,\alpha\neq 0\}\text{.}\)
  4. Conclude that the projective linear group \(PGL(V):=GL(V)/K\) acts on \(\Proj(V)\text{.}\)
