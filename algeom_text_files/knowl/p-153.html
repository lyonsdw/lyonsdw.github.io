A sequence of groups and homomorphisms

\begin{equation*} \cdots \underset{f_{i-2}}{\longrightarrow} G_{i-1} \underset{f_{i-1}}{\longrightarrow} G_{i} \underset{f_{i}}{\longrightarrow} G_{i+1} \underset{f_{i+1}}{\longrightarrow}\cdots \end{equation*}

is exact if \(\ker(f_{i})=\im(f_{i-1})\) for all \(i\text{.}\) A short exact sequence of groups and homomorphisms is an exact sequence of the form

\begin{equation*} 1\to K\to G\to H\to 1 \end{equation*}

where \(1\) denotes the trivial group. A short exact sequence

\begin{equation} 1{\to} K \underset{i}{\longrightarrow} G \underset{\pi}{\longrightarrow} H\to 1\tag{1.6.1} \end{equation}

is said to \(split\) if there is a group homomorphism \(s\colon H\to G\) such that \(\pi\circ s\) is the identity homomorphism on \(H\text{.}\)

  1. Show that for a short exact sequence of the form (1.6.1), that the homomorphism \(i\) is one-to-one, the homomorphism \(\pi\) is onto, and \(G/K\approx H\text{.}\)
  2. Suppose that (1.6.1) splits, with splitting \(s\colon H\to G\text{.}\) Show that \(\phi\colon H\to \Aut(K)\) given by
    \begin{equation*} \phi(h)(k) = i^{-1}\left(f(h)i(k)f(h)^{-1}\right) \end{equation*}
    is defined 2  and is a homomorphism.
  3. Show that the short exact sequence (1.6.1) splits, with splitting \(s\text{,}\) if and only if \(G\approx K\times_{\phi} H\text{,}\) where \(\phi\) is described in the previous part above.
