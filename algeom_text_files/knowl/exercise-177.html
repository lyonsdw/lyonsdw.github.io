Exercise 6. Identifications of \(U(\Quat)\) and \(\S\) with \(\Rot(S^2)\).

The discussion of elliptic geometry (Section 3.4) establishes two ways to construct rotations from matrices. The purpose of this exercise is to reconcile these identifications. Given \(a,b\in \C\) with \(|a|^2+|b|^2=1\text{,}\) let us define the objects

\begin{equation*} M_{a,b}\in SU(2),T_{a,b}\in \S,R_{T_{a,b}}\in \Rot(S^2),r_{a,b}\in U(\Quat),R_{r_{a,b}}\in \Rot(S_\Quat^2) \end{equation*}

as follows.

\begin{align*} M_{a,b} \amp =\left[\begin{array}{cc}{a}\amp {b}\\{-b^\ast}\amp {a^\ast}\end{array}\right]\\ T_{a,b} \amp = \left[z\to \frac{az+b}{-b^\ast z+a^\ast}\right] \;\;\text{for }z\in \extC\\ R_{T_{a,b}} \amp = s^{-1}\circ T\circ s\\ r_{a,b}\amp = a+bi+cj+dk \\ R_{r_{a,b}}\amp = \left[u\to r_{a,b}ur_{a,b}^\ast\right]\;\; \text{for }u\in \S^2_\Quat \end{align*}

Let's call the following sequence of mappings the "Möbius path" from \(SU(2)\) to \(\Rot(S^2)\)

\begin{align} SU(2)\amp \to \S \to \Rot(S^2)\tag{3.6.3}\\ M_{a,b} \amp \to T_{a,b} \to R_{T_{a,b}}\tag{3.6.4} \end{align}

and let's call this next sequence the "quaternion path".

\begin{align} SU(2)\amp \to U(\Quat) \to \Rot(S^2_\Quat)\tag{3.6.5}\\ M_{a,b} \amp \to r_{a,b} \to R_{r_{a,b}}\tag{3.6.6} \end{align}
  1. Show that \(R_{T_{a,b}} = R_{v,\theta}\) with \(v=(\im(b),-\re(b),\im(a))\) and \(\theta =2\arccos(a)\text{.}\)
  2. Show that \(R_{r_{a,b}} = R_{v,\theta}\) with \(v=(\im(a),\re(b),\im(b))\) and \(\theta =2\arccos(a)\text{.}\)
  3. Here is one way to reconcile the mismatch of the axis vectors in parts (a) and (b). Let
    \begin{equation*} M_{a',b'} = (iH)M_{a,b}(iH)^{-1} \end{equation*}
    where \(H=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\left[\begin{array}{cc} 1\amp 1 \\ 1\amp -1\end{array}\right]\) is the Hadamard matrix. Show that
    \begin{equation*} R_{T_{a,b}}= R_{r_{a',b'}} \end{equation*}
    that is, the diagram in Figure 3.6.1 commutes.
