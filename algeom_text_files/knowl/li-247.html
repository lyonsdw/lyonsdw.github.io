Item b.

It is clear that the product of two even permutations is an even permutation. If \(\sigma=\tau_1\cdots \tau_r\) is a product of transpositions, then \(\sigma^{-1}=\tau_r\cdots \tau_1\text{,}\) so the inverse of an even permutation is also an even permutation. The set \(A_n\) is not empty because the identity is an even permutation. We conclude that \(A_n\) is a subgroup of \(S_n\) by the 2-step subgroup test.
