A map \(\phi\colon G\to H\) is called a homomorphism if

\begin{equation*} \phi(xy) = \phi(x)\phi(y) \end{equation*}

for all \(x,y\) in \(G\text{.}\) A homomorphism that is both injective (one-to-one) and surjective (onto) is called an isomorphism of groups. \(\phi\colon G\to H\) is an isomorphism, we say that \(G\) is isomorphic to \(H\text{,}\) and we write \(G\approx H\text{.}\)
