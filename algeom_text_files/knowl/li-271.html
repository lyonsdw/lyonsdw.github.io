These are both 3-4-5 triangles. One way to map the first triangle to the second is the following sequence of transformations.
  1. shift left 6 units: \((x,y)\to (x-6,y)\)
  2. rotate 90 degrees (counterclockwise): \((x,y)\to (-y,x)\)
  3. shift down 1 unit: \((x,y)\to (x,y-1)\)
  4. reflect across the \(y\)-axis: \((x,y)\to (-x,y)\)
Putting all this together, we get \((x,y)\to (y,x-7)\text{.}\) This is the Euclidean transformation \(v\to Rv + b\) with \(R=\twotwo{0}{1}{1}{0}\) and \(b=(0,-7)\text{.}\)
