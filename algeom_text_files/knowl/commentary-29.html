First we consider the "if" direction. Suppose \(f\colon X\to Y\) is constant on equivalence classes of the equivalence relation \(\sim\) on \(X\text{.}\) It is clear then that \(\overline{f}([x])=f(x)\) is well-defined. For the "only if" direction, suppose that \(\overline{f}\colon X\to Y\) given by \(\overline{f}([x])=f(x)\) exists. Now if \(x\sim y\text{,}\) we have \([x]=[y]\text{,}\) so \(f(x)=\overline{f}([x])=\overline{f}([y])=f(y)\text{.}\) It follows that \(f\) is constant on equivalence classes.
