Item d.

Let \(C\) make an angle \(t\) with the positive real axis, and let \(a\) be a point on \(C\text{.}\) Let \(Sz=e^{-it}(z-a)\text{,}\) so that \(S(C)=\R\text{.}\) Applying PropositionÂ 3.2.16, we have \((Sz)^{\ast S(C)} = (Sz)^\ast = e^{it}(z-a)^\ast\text{.}\) Setting this last expression equal to \(S(z^{\ast C}\) and applying \(S^{-1}\) to both sides, we have
\begin{equation*} z^{\ast C} = e^{i2t}(z-a)^\ast + a. \end{equation*}
We know that \(Sz,(Sz)^\ast\) are reflections of one another across the real axis. Because \(S^{-1}\) is a rigid motion (rotation followed by a translation, so distances and angles are preserved), the pair \(z,z^{\ast C}\) must be reflections of each other across the line \(C\text{.}\)
