Let \((X,G)\text{,}\) \((Y,H)\text{,}\) and \((Z,K)\) be geometries. Clearly \((X,G)\) is equivalent to itself via the identity map on \(X\text{.}\) It is also easy to see that if \(\mu\colon X\to Y\) is a geometry equivalence map, then \(\mu^{-1}\colon Y\to X\) is also a geometry equivalence. It is also easy to see that if \(\mu\colon X\to Y\) and \(\nu\colon Y\to Z\) are geometry equivalence maps, then \(\nu\circ \mu\colon X\to Z\) is also a geometry equivalence map.
