To convert from polar to rectangular, use the equations \(x=r\cos \theta, y=r\sin \theta\) (show sketches to explain these formulas). To convert from rectangular to polar, use \(r=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}\) and \(\tan \theta = y/x\text{.}\) For the last equation, you must use judgment when \(x=0\) to decide whether \(\theta\) should be \(\pi/2\) or \(-\pi/2\text{.}\) You must also use judgment when calculating \(\theta = \arctan (y/x)\text{.}\) The standard codomain for \(\arctan\) is the interval \((\pi/2,\pi/2)\text{,}\) so you need to use \(\theta = \arctan (y/x) + \pi\) for \(x\lt 0\text{.}\)
