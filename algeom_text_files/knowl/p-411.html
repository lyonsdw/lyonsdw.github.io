In the case where \(T\) has one fixed point \(p\text{,}\) the conjugating map \(Sz=\frac{1}{z-p}\) sends \(p\to \infty\text{,}\) so \(S^{-1}\) maps \(\infty \to p\text{,}\) and \(S^{-1}\) maps lines in the \(w\)-plane that are parallel to the line through \(0\) and \(\beta\) to clines in the \(z\)-plane that contain \(p\text{.}\) Every cline in this family is tangent to every other cline in this family at exactly the one point \(p\text{.}\) Clines in this family are called degenerate Steiner circles.
