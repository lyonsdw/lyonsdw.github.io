Proof

By Lemma 3.2.9, there exists a Möbius transformation \(U\) that maps \(z_1,z_2,z_3\) to \(1,0,\infty\text{,}\) and there is a Möbius transformation \(V\) that maps \(w_1,w_2,w_3\) to \(1,0,\infty\text{.}\) It is easy to check that \(T=V^{-1}U\) satisfies \(Tz_i=w_i\) for \(i=1,2,3\text{.}\) Now suppose that there is another Möbius transformation \(S\) such that \(Sz_i=w_i\) for \(i=1,2,3\text{.}\) It is easy to check that \(S\circ T^{-1}\) fixes three points. By Lemma 3.2.10, we have \(S\circ T^{-1}=\Id\text{,}\) so \(S=T\text{.}\)
