A triangle with one vertex in \(\D\) and two vertices on the unit circle, connected by arcs of circles that are orthogonal to the unit circle, is called a doubly-asymptotic hyperbolic triangle. Examples are \(\triangle AA_1A_2\text{,}\) \(\triangle BB_1B_2\text{,}\) and \(\triangle CC_1C_2\) in Figure 3.3.22.

  2. Now use the integration formula for the upper half-plane model to show that the area of the doubly-asymptotic triangle with angle \(\alpha\) (at the vertex interior to \(\U\)) is \(\pi-\alpha\text{.}\)
