Item a.

Let \({\mathcal E}, {\mathcal O}\) denote the set of even, or odd, respectively, elements of \(S_n\text{.}\) Let \(\tau\) be a transposition (\(\tau=(12)\) will do fine). Notice that \(L_\tau\colon S_n\to \S_n\) given by \(L_\tau(\sigma)=\tau\sigma\) is a bijective map (its inverse is itself) and takes \({\mathcal E}\) to \({\mathcal O}\text{,}\) and vice-versa. Thus it must be the case that \(|{\mathcal E}|=|{\mathcal O}|\text{.}\)
