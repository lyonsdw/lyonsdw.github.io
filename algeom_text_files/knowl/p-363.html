Let \(\mu\colon \R^2\to \C\) be the canonical identification \(\mu(x,y)=x+iy\text{.}\) All three of the types of generators for \({\mathcal E}_2\) are conjugate to elements of \({\mathcal E}_1\text{,}\) as follows.

\begin{align*} (\mu^{-1}\circ [z\to e^{itz}]\circ \mu) \amp = \left[v\to \twotwo{\cos t}{-\sin t}{\sin t}{\cos t}v\right]\in {\mathcal E}_1\\ (\mu^{-1} \circ [z\to z+b] \circ \mu) \amp= \left[v\to v+(\re(b),\im(b))\right]\in {\mathcal E}_1\\ (\mu^{-1} \circ [z\to z^\ast] \circ \mu) \amp= \left[(x,y)\to v+(x,-y))\right]\in {\mathcal E}_1 \end{align*}

We can also read these conjugation relations in reverse.

\begin{align*} (\mu^{-1}\circ \left[v\to \twotwo{\cos t}{-\sin t}{\sin t}{\cos t}v\right]\circ \mu) \amp = [z\to e^{itz}]\in {\mathcal E}_2\\ (\mu^{-1} \circ \left[v\to v+(\re(b),\im(b))\right] \circ \mu) \amp= [z\to z+b]\in {\mathcal E}_2\\ (\mu^{-1} \circ \left[(x,y)\to v+(x,-y))\right]\circ \mu) \amp= [z\to z^\ast]\in {\mathcal E}_2 \end{align*}

Using the fact that \(O(2)\) is generated by matrices of the form \(\twotwo{\cos t}{-\sin t}{\sin t}{\cos t}\) and \(\twotwo{1}{0}{0}{-1}\text{,}\) we have shown that generators of \({\mathcal E}_1\) are conjugate to generators of \({\mathcal E}_2\text{,}\) and vice-versa. It is easy to see that, because the conjugacy condition holds for generators, the conjugacy condition holds for all of \({\mathcal E}_1\) and \({\mathcal E}_2.\)
