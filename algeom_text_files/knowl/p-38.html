Let \(C\) be the circle of radius \(r\gt 0\) and with center \(a\in \C\text{.}\) A point \(z\) lies on \(C\) if and only if the distance from \(z\) to \(a\) equals \(r\text{.}\) In mathematical symbols, \(C\) is the set of solutions \(z\) for the following equation.
\begin{equation} |z-a|=r\tag{1.1.22} \end{equation}
