The conjugate of the complex number \(z=x+iy=re^{i\theta}\text{,}\) denoted \(\overline{z}\) or \(z^\ast\) , is defined to be \(z^\ast = x-iy = re^{-i\theta}\text{.}\) Geometrically, \(z^\ast\) is the reflection of \(z\) across the real axis (the \(x\)-axis) in \(\R^2\text{.}\) Here are some relations involving conjugates. For all \(z,w\in \C\text{,}\) we have the following.
\begin{align} \re(z) \amp = \frac{z + z^\ast}{2}\tag{1.1.16}\\ \im(z) \amp = \frac{z - z^\ast}{2i}\tag{1.1.17}\\ |z|^2 \amp = zz^\ast\tag{1.1.18}\\ 2\arg z \amp = \frac{z}{z^\ast} \;\;\text{(for $z\neq 0$)}\tag{1.1.19}\\ \frac{1}{z} \amp = \frac{z^\ast}{zz^\ast}= \frac{z^\ast}{|z|^2} \;\;\text{(for $z\neq 0$)}\tag{1.1.20}\\ (zw)^\ast \amp = z^\ast w^\ast\tag{1.1.21} \end{align}
