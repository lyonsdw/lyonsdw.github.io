Geometry is the study of shape and properties of configurations of points. A central concept is that of congruence. In his 1872 work called the Erlangen Programm, Felix Klein unified various notions of geometry using transormation groups of congruences. Here is a version of Klein's definition.

Definition 2.1.1. Geometry.
A geometry model is a pair \((G,X)\text{,}\) where \(G\) is a group acting on a set \(X\text{,}\) called the (model) space. Subsets of \(X\) are called figures. Figures \(F,F'\) are called congruent if there is a transformation \(g\) in \(G\) such that \(g(F)=F'\text{.}\)
To introduce what this means, we begin with a comparison of properties of line segments and angles in a flat plane versus lines segments and angles on the surface of a sphere. For now, we appeal to the intuition and avoid technical definitions.
