First, we solve \(w=\frac{az+b}{cz+d}\) for \(z\text{.}\) This yields \(z=\frac{dw-b}{-cw+a}\text{.}\) When we check the composition, we see that
\begin{align*} \frac{a\left(\frac{dw-b}{-cw+a}\right)+b}{c\left(\frac{dw-b}{-cw+a}\right)+d} \amp = \frac{a(dw-b)+b(-cw+a)}{c(dw-b)+d(-cw+a)}\\ \amp = w\frac{ad-bc}{ad-bc}. \end{align*}
Thus it is clear that the condition \(ad-bc\) is necessary and sufficient for invertibility.
