Informally and intuitively, we say that regular polygons have rotational and mirror symmetries. Specifically, the rotational symmetries are rotations about the center \(O\) of the polygon, clockwise or counterclockwise, by some angle \(\angle POP'\text{,}\) where \(P,P'\) are any two vertices. The mirror symmetries of the polygon are reflections across lines of the form \(\overline{OP}\) or \(\overline{OM}\text{,}\) where \(P\) is any vertex and \(M\) is the midpoint of any edge of the polygon.
