Exercise 2. The group of units in \(\Z_n\).

Let \(U_n\) denote the group of elements in \(Z_n\) that have multiplicative inverses, that is,

\begin{equation*} U_n = \{x\in \Z_n\colon \exists y, xy=1\pmod{n}\}. \end{equation*}
  1. Show that \(x\) is in \(U_n\) if and only if \(x\) is relatively prime to \(n\text{.}\) [Comment: The function \(n\to |U_n|\text{,}\) important in number theory, is called Euler's phi function, written \(\phi(n)=|U_n|\text{.}\)]
  2. Show that \(U_n\) with the binary operation of multiplication mod \(n\) is an Abelian group. [Comment: The group \(U_n\) is called the the group of (multiplicative) units in \(\Z_n\text{.}\)]
  3. Show that \(U_n\) is isomorphic to \(\Aut(\Z_n)\) via \(x\to [a\to ax]\text{.}\)
