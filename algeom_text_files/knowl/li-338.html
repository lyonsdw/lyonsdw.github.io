Item b.

Solving \(Tz=z\text{,}\) we get \((z-1)^2=0\text{,}\) so \(T\) has a single (degenerate) fixed point at \(1\text{.}\) The normal form is
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{Tz-1} = \frac{1}{z-1}+\beta. \end{equation*}
Putting \(z=0\) and solving for \(\beta\text{,}\) we get \(\beta=1/2\text{.}\) It is clear that \(T\) takes the real axis to itself, so degenerate Steiner circles are tangent to the real axis at \(1\text{.}\)
