Given a complex number \(z\) corresponding to the point \((x,y)\) in \(\R^2\text{,}\) we say that \(x\) is the real part of \(z\) and that \(y\) is the imaginary part of \(z\text{,}\) denoted \(\re(z)=x\) and \(\im(z)=y\text{.}\) The set \(\C\) contains the set \(\R\) as a subset. The real number \(x\text{,}\) which is also the complex number \(x\text{,}\) corresponds to the ordered pair \((x,0)\text{.}\) A complex number that corresponds to an ordered pair \((0,y)\) is called (pure) imaginary.
