Let \(X\) be the set
\begin{equation*} X=\Z\times(\Z\setminus\{0\})=\{(m,n)\colon m,n\in \Z, n\neq 0\}. \end{equation*}
Define the relation \(\sim\) on \(X\) by
\begin{equation*} (m,n)\sim (m',n')\Leftrightarrow mn' = m'n. \end{equation*}
Let \(f\colon X\to \Z\) be given by
\begin{equation*} f(m,n)=m^2+n^2 \text{.} \end{equation*}
Let \(g\colon X\to \Q\) (where \(\Q\) denotes the rational numbers \(\Q=\{\frac{m}{n}\colon m,n\in \Z,n\neq 0\}\)) be given by
\begin{equation*} g(m,n)=\frac{m}{n} \text{.} \end{equation*}
5.
Show that \(\sim\) is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive.
6.
Draw a sketch of \(X\) showing the partition \(\{[(m,n)]\colon (m,n)\in X\}\text{.}\)
7.
  1. Is the map \(\overline{f}\colon X/\sim \to \Z\) given by \(\overline{f}([(m,n)])=f(m,n)\) well-defined? Explain.
  2. Draw a sketch of \(X\) that shows the equivalence classes \(X/{\sim_f}\text{.}\)
8.
  1. Is the map \(\overline{g}\colon X/\sim \to \Q\) given by \(\overline{g}([(m,n)])=g(m,n)\) well-defined? Explain.
  2. Draw a sketch of \(X\) that shows the equivalence classes \(X/{\sim_g}\text{.}\)
9.
Explain why \(\sim\) and \(\sim_g\) are the same equivalence relation on \(X\text{.}\) Explain why \(X/\sim\) is in one-to-one correspondence with the rational numbers \(\Q\text{.}\)
