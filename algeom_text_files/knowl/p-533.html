The matrices \(M_{Z,\theta},M_{X,\pi/2}\) meet the criteria of membership in \(SU(2)\) with \(a=e^{i\pi/2}, b=0\) for \(M_{Z,\theta}\) and and \(a=1/\sqrt{2},b=i/\sqrt{2}\) for \(M_{X,\pi/2}\text{.}\) That is, the entries have the form \(\twotwo{a}{b}{-b^\ast}{a^\ast}\) and satisfy \(|a|^2+|b|^2=1\text{.}\) The fact that Equations (3.4.3) and (3.4.4) hold is a byproduct of Exercises Exercise 1.3.3.4 and Exercise 1.3.3.6, respectively. To see why every element in \(\S\) is the image under \({\mathcal T}\) of an element of \(SU(2)\text{,}\) let \(S\in \S\text{,}\) and write \(S\) as a composition \(S=T_1\circ T_2 \circ \cdots \circ T_k\text{,}\) where each \(T_i\) is of the form form of one of \(T_{Z,\theta},T_{X,\pi/2}\text{.}\) Then we have
\begin{align*} S\amp ={\mathcal T}(M_1)\circ {\mathcal T}(M_2) \circ \cdots \circ {\mathcal T}(M_k)\\ \amp = {\mathcal T}(M_1M_2\cdots M_k) \end{align*}
where each \(M_i\) is of the form of one of \(M_{Z,\theta},M_{X,\pi/2}\text{.}\)
