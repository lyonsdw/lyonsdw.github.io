Let
\begin{equation*} Tz = \frac{az+b}{-\overline{b}z+\overline{a}} \end{equation*}
with \(|a|^2+|b|^2=1\text{.}\) The fixed points of \(T\) are given by
\begin{equation*} p,q = \frac{a-\overline{a} \pm \sqrt{(a-\overline{a})^2 -4|b|^2}}{-2\overline{b}} = \frac{a-\overline{a} \pm \sqrt{(a+\overline{a})^2 -4}}{-2\overline{b}} . \end{equation*}
Putting \(z=\infty\text{,}\) \(Tz=T(\infty) = a/(-\overline{b})\) in the normal form equation (3.2.6), we get
\begin{equation*} \lambda = \frac{a+p\overline{b}}{a+q\overline{b}} = \frac{a+\overline{a}+\sqrt{(a+\overline{a})^2 -4}}{a+\overline{a}-\sqrt{(a+\overline{a})^2 -4}}. \end{equation*}
The last expression has the form \((c+di)/(c-di)\) for \(c,d\) real, so we conclude \(|\alpha|=1\text{.}\)
