  1. We have \(a(-d/c)+b = -\frac{ad-bc}{c}\neq 0\) (by assumption), so \(ax+b\to -\frac{ad-bc}{c}\neq 0\) and \(cx+d \to 0\) as \(x\to -d/c\text{,}\) so \(|f(x)|\to \infty\) as \(x\to -d/c\text{.}\)

  2. We have

    \begin{equation*} \frac{ax+b}{cx+d}=\frac{a}{c}\left(\frac{1+\frac{b}{ax}}{1+\frac{d}{cx}}\right). \end{equation*}
    It is clear that the latter expression tends to \(a/c\) as \(x\to \infty\text{.}\)
