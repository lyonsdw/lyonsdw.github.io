Exercise 7. The order of a group element.
Let \(g\) be an element of a group \(G\text{.}\) The order of \(g\text{,}\) denoted \(|g|\text{,}\) is the smallest positive integer \(n\) such that \(g^n=e\text{,}\) if such an integer exists. If there is no positive integer \(n\) such that \(g^n=e\text{,}\) then \(g\) is said to have infinite order. Show that
\begin{equation*} |g|=|\langle g\rangle|\text{.} \end{equation*}
