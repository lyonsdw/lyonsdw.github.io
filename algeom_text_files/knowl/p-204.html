  • Planar Euclidean geometry. The model space for planar Euclidean geometry is the plane \(\R^2\text{.}\) The group of congruences consists of translations, rotations, and reflections. Specifically, Euclidean congruences are functions of the form \(\vec{v}\to R\vec{v}+\vec{b}\text{,}\) where \(\vec{v} \in \R^2\text{,}\) \(R\) is an element of the group of \(2\times 2\) orthogonal matrices, and \(\vec{b}\in\R^2\text{.}\)
  • Spherical geometry. The model space for spherical geometry is the sphere \(S^2=\{(x,y,z)\in \R^3\colon x^2+y^2+z^2=1\}\text{.}\) The group of congruences consists of rotations of the sphere and reflections across planes through the origin. Specifically, spherical congruences are functions of the form \(\vec{v}\to R\vec{v}\text{,}\) where \(\vec{v} \in \R^3\text{,}\) \(|\vec{v}|=1\text{,}\) and \(R\) is an element of the group of \(3\times 3\) orthogonal matrices.
  • Projective geometry. The model space for a projective geometry is projective space \(\Proj(V)\text{,}\) where \(V\) is a vector space \(V\) (see ExerciseÂ 2.5.2.4 in the previous chapter). The group of congruence transformations is the projective linear group \(PGL(V)\text{.}\)
