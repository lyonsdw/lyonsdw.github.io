Let \(C_1,C_2\) be two curves, parameterized by \(p(t),q(s)\text{,}\) respectively, intersecting at \(v=p(0)=q(0)\text{,}\) and with

\begin{equation*} \theta=\lim_{s\to 0^+,t\to 0^+} \arg\left(\frac{q(s)-v}{p(t)-v}\right) \end{equation*}

one of the four oriented angles made by lines tangent to \(C_1,C_2\) at \(v\text{.}\) After transforming the curves by inversion, the corresponding angle is

\begin{align*} \lim_{s\to 0^+,t\to 0^+} \arg\left(\frac{\frac{1}{q(s)}-\frac{1}{v}}{\frac{1}{p(t)}-\frac{1}{v}}\right) \amp = \lim_{s\to 0^+,t\to 0^+} \arg\left(\frac{\frac{v-q(s)}{vq(s)v}}{\frac{v-p(t)}{vp(t)}}\right)\\ \amp = \lim_{s\to 0^+,t\to 0^+} \arg\left(\frac{p(t)}{q(s)}\frac{v-q(s)}{v-p(t)}\right)\\ \amp = \lim_{s\to 0^+,t\to 0^+} \arg\left(\frac{q(s)-v}{p(t)-v}\right)\\ \amp = \theta \end{align*}

(\(q(s)/p(t)\to 1\) because \(q(s), p(t)\to v\) as \(s,t\to 0\)).
