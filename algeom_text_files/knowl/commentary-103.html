Instructor’s solution for Checkpoint 3.2.19.

Suppose \((z_0,z_1,z_2,z_3)\) is real. Let \(T=(\cdot,z_1,z_2,z_3)\text{,}\) so that \(z_0\in T^{-1}(\extR)\text{.}\) Applying Proposition 3.2.17, we conclude that \(z_0\) lies on the unique cline through \(z_1,z_2,z_3\text{.}\) Conversely, suppose that \(z_0,z_1,z_2,z_3\) lie on a cline \(C\text{.}\) Again, let \(T=(\cdot,z_1,z_2,z_3)\text{.}\) By Proposition 3.2.17, we have \(C=T^{-1}(\extR)\text{,}\) so it must be that \(Tz_0=(z_0,z_1,z_2,z_3)\) is real.
