Checkpoint 0.2.3.

Show that \((x,y)\to (x,-y)\) is a lift of \(x\to 1/x\text{.}\) Find \(s^{-1}(3)\text{.}\) It is intuitively clear that stereographic projection is a bijection. Make this rigorous by finding a formula for the inverse \(s^{-1}\colon \R^+\to S^1\) and checking that \(s\circ s^{-1}\) is the identity of \(\R^+\) and that \(s^{-1}\circ s\) is the identity on \(S^1\text{.}\)
