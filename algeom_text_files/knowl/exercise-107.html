Exercise 1. Warm up exercises with the three example geometries.
  1. Find the Euclidean congruence transformation that takes the triangle with vertices \((2,0),(6,0),(6,3)\) to the triangle with vertices \((0,-1),(0,-5),(3,-5)\text{.}\)
  2. Find the spherical congruence that takes the three points \((0,0,1),(0,0,-1),(1,0,0)\) to the three points \((1,0,0),(-1,0,0),(0,\cos\theta,\sin\theta)\) (in that order).
  3. Find the projective transformation in \(PGL(2,\C)\) that takes the three points \([(1,1)],[(0,1)],[(1,0)]\) in \(\Proj(\C^2)\)to \([(a,1),(b,1),(c,1)]\) (in that order).
  4. Verify that the Euclidean transformation group described above is indeed a group by finding the composition of two transformations and the inverse of a transformation.
  5. Let \(d(P,Q)\) denote the distance between points \(P,Q\) in Euclidean geometry, and let \(T\) be a Euclidean congruence transformation. Show that \(d(T(P),T(Q))=d(P,Q)\text{.}\)
