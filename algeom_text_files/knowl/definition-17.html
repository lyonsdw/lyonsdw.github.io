Definition 2.1.1. Geometry.
A geometry model is a pair \((G,X)\text{,}\) where \(G\) is a group acting on a set \(X\text{,}\) called the (model) space. Subsets of \(X\) are called figures. Figures \(F,F'\) are called congruent if there is a transformation \(g\) in \(G\) such that \(g(F)=F'\text{.}\)
