Item 3.3.6.4.c.
Let \(p\in \D\) lie on a hyperbolic line \(L\text{,}\) let \(q\in \D\text{,}\) let \(M\) be a line through \(q\) perpendicular to \(L\) (this line \(M\) exists by Exercise 3.3.6.3), and let \(q'\) be the point of intersection of \(L,M\text{.}\) Show that \(d(p,q')\leq d(p,q)\text{.}\) Suggestion: apply \(T\in \H\) that takes \(p\to 0\) and takes \(L\to \R\text{.}\) Let \(t=-\arg(Tq)\) if \(\re(Tq)\geq 0\) and let \(t=\pi-\arg(Tq)\) if \(\re(Tq)\lt 0\text{.}\) Let \(r=e^{it}Tq\text{.}\) See Figure 3.3.21.
