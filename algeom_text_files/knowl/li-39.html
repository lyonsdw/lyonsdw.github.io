Item b.
We have
\begin{align*} R_r(u)\amp =(\cos\theta + u\sin\theta)v(\cos\theta + u\sin\theta)^\ast\\ \amp = (\cos\theta + u\sin\theta)(v\cos\theta -vu\sin\theta)\\ \amp = v\cos^2\theta + (uv-vu)\sin\theta\cos\theta) -uvu\sin^2\theta\\ \amp =v(\cos^2\theta -\sin^2\theta) + 2w\sin\theta\cos\theta \end{align*}
(use (1.2.12) to simplify \(uv-vu=2u\times v=2w\) and use ExerciseÂ 1.2.6.1 to get \(v=uvu\text{,}\) the reflection of \(v\) in the plane perpendicular to \(u\)). Using double angle formulas, the last expression in the displayed derivation becomes
\begin{equation*} v\cos(2\theta) + w\sin(2\theta). \end{equation*}
Since \(R_r\) is a linear map that acts on the an orthogonal basis \(u,v,w\) as a rotation about \(u\) by the angle \(2\theta\text{,}\) the Proposition is established.
