A circle in \(S^2\) is a circle in a plane intersecting \(S^2\text{.}\) A great circle is the intersection of \(S^2\) with a plane through the origin. In elliptic geometry, a great circle is called an elliptic straight line because the path of shortest length connecting two given points in \(S^2\) is an arc of a great circle. Circles in \(S^2\) that are not great circles are called elliptic cycles.
