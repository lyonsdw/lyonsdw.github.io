No. Let \((X,G)\) be a geometry and let \(H\) be any group, and consider the action of \(G\times H\) on \(X\) given by \((g,h)x = gx\text{.}\) Then \((X,G)\) and \((X,G\times H)\) are equivalent via the identity map on \(X\text{,}\) but unless \(H\) is trivial, \(G\) is not isomorphic to \(G\times H\text{.}\)
