Let \(r=\cos\theta + u\sin\theta\) be the polar form for a unit quaternion \(r\text{.}\)

  1. Show that \(R_r(u)=u\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(v\) be any pure unit quaternion orthogonal to \(u\text{,}\) and let \(w=u\times v\text{,}\) so that the triple \(u,v,w\) forms a right-handed coordinate system for \(\R^3\text{.}\) Show that
    \begin{equation*} R_r(v) = \cos(2\theta)v + \sin(2\theta)w \end{equation*}
    (use equation (1.2.13)) and explain how this proves the Proposition.
