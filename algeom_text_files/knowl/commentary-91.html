Instructor's solution for Exercise 3.1.4.4.

Given parameterizations \(p(t),q(s)\) for curves \(C_1,C_2\) that intersect at \(v=p(0)=q(0)\text{,}\) the limit (3.1.2) gives an oriented angle \(\theta\) between the lines tangent to the curve at \(v\text{.}\) Figure 3.1.8 illustrates the case when \(\theta\lt 0\text{.}\) The four possible combinations of one-sided limits \(t\to 0^{\pm}, s\to 0^{\pm}\) have the two possible values \(\theta, \theta+\pi\text{.}\) For the case \(\theta\gt 0\text{,}\) the two possible values of (3.1.2) (with four possible one-sided limits) are \(\theta, \theta-\pi\text{.}\)
