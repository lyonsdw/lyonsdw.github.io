Instructor's solution for ExerciseÂ 3.4.7.3.

We have

\begin{align*} \text{Area}(R_{\alpha}) \amp = \int_0^\alpha \int_0^\infty \frac{4r\;dr\;d\theta}{(1+r^2)^2}\\ \amp = 2\int_0^\alpha \left[\frac{du}{u}\right]_1^\infty d\theta \;\;(u=1+r^2)\\ \amp = 2\int_0^\alpha 1 \; d\theta\\ \amp = 2\alpha. \end{align*}

You can see this is the right answer on \(S^2\text{.}\) The area of \(R_\alpha\) is the fraction \(\frac{\alpha}{2\pi}\) of the total area \(4\pi\) of \(S^2\text{,}\) so

\begin{equation*} \text{Area}(R_\alpha) = \frac{\alpha}{2\pi}4\pi=2\alpha. \end{equation*}
