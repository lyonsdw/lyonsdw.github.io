Exercise 1. Angles and conformal transformations.

The complex plane comes with a measure of oriented angle built in. If \(u\) is a postive real number, \(v=0\text{,}\) and \(w\neq 0\) is a complex number, the measure of the oriented angle \(\angle uvw\) is \(\arg w\text{.}\) More generally, if \(u,v,w\) are three complex numbers with \(v\) distinct from \(u\) and \(w\text{,}\) the measure of the oriented angle \(\angle uvw\) is \(\arg\left(\frac{w-v}{u-v}\right)\) (see Figure blah).
