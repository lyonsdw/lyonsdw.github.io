Item c.

We need a two-by-two invertible matrix \(T\) such that \(T(1,0)= \alpha(c,1)\text{,}\) \(T(0,1)=\beta(b,1)\) and \(T(1,1)= \gamma(a,1)\) for some \(\alpha,\beta,\gamma\text{.}\) The first two requirements are met by \(T=\twotwo{\alpha c}{\beta b}{\alpha}{\beta}\text{.}\) Solve the system \(T(1,1)=\gamma(a,1)\) leads to \((\alpha,\beta)\propto (a-b,c-a)\text{.}\) This leads to the projective transformation given by the matrix \(\twotwo{(a-b)c}{(c-a)b}{a-b}{c-a}\text{.}\)
