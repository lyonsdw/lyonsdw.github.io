Instructor’s solution for Checkpoint 3.2.14.

Solving \(z=\frac{az+b}{cz+d}\text{,}\) we have \(cz^2+z(d-a)-b=0\text{.}\) If \(c\neq 0\text{,}\) we have 1 or 2 solutions
\begin{equation*} z=\frac{a-d\pm\sqrt{(d-a)^2+4bc}}{2c}. \end{equation*}
If \(c=0\) and \(d\neq a\text{,}\) we have two fixed points, \(z=b/(d-a),\infty\text{.}\) If \(c=0\text{,}\) \(d=a\text{,}\) and \(b\neq 0\text{,}\) then \(\infty\) is the only fixed point. Finally, if \(c=0\text{,}\) \(d=a\text{,}\) and \(b=0\text{,}\) then \(Tz=z\text{.}\)
