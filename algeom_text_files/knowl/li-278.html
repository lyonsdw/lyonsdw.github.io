Item d.

Let \(T_1,T_2\) be Euclidean transformations given by \(v\to R_1v+b_1\) and \(v\to R_2v+b_2\text{,}\) respectively. The composition
\begin{equation*} T_2(T_1(v))=R_2(R_1v+b_1)+ b_2=R_2R_1v + (R_2b_1+b_2) \end{equation*}
is indeed a Euclidean transformation. The identity element has \(R=\Id\) and \(b=(0,0)\text{.}\) The inverse of \(v\to Rv+b\) is given by \(v\to R^{-1}v - R^{-1}b\text{.}\)
