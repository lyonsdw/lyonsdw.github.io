Instructor's solution for Exercise 3.2.6.9.

Let \(T\) be a Möbius transformation with two fixed points \(p,q\text{.}\) Let \(w=Sz=\frac{z-p}{z-q}\text{,}\) and let \(STS^{-1}w = \alpha w\text{.}\) Steiner circles of the second kind are, by definition, sets of the form \(S^{-1}(\{|w|=r\})\) for some \(r\gt 0\text{.}\) Thus a Steiner circle of the second kind is the set of \(z\) that satisfy

\begin{equation*} \frac{|z-p|}{|z-q|}=\frac{|r|}{|\alpha|}. \end{equation*}

Setting \(P=z\text{,}\) \(A=p\text{,}\) \(Q=b\text{,}\) and \(k=\frac{|r|}{|\alpha|}\text{,}\) we see that a Steiner circle of the second kind for \(T\) has the form

\begin{equation} \left\{P\colon \frac{d(P,A)}{d(Q,B)}=k\right\}.\tag{3.2.13} \end{equation}

Conversely, given any \(A,B\in \C\) with \(A\neq B\) and any \(k\gt 0\text{,}\) let \(p=A\text{,}\) \(q=B\text{,}\) and \(\alpha\neq 0,1\) be chosen arbitrarily. Let \(Sz=\frac{z-p}{z-q}\text{,}\) and solve \(STS^{-1}=\mu_\alpha\) (where \(\mu_\alpha\) denotes the map \(w\to \alpha w\)) for \(T\text{.}\) We have \(T=S^{-1}\mu_\alpha S\text{.}\) By the preceding discussion, we have

\begin{equation*} S^{-1}(\{|w|=|\alpha|k\}) = \left\{P\colon \frac{d(P,A)}{d(Q,B)}=k\right\} \end{equation*}

so that the set (3.2.13) is a Steiner circle of the second kind for \(T\text{.}\)
