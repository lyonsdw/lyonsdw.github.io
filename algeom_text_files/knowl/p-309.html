Let \(H\) be a subgroup of a group \(G\text{,}\) and consider the map

\begin{equation*} R\colon H\to \Perm(G) \end{equation*}

given by \(h\to R_h\text{,}\) where \(R_h(g)=gh^{-1}\) (this is the restriction of right inverse multiplication action in ExerciseÂ 2.5.3.1 to \(H\)). Show that the orbits of this action of \(H\) on \(G\) are the same as the cosets of \(H\text{.}\) This shows that the two potentially different meanings of \(G/H\) (one is the set of cosets, the other is the set of orbits of the action of \(H\) on \(G\) via \(R\)), are in fact in agreement.
