Definition 2.3.1. Subgroups and cosets.

A subset \(H\) of a group \(G\) is called a subgroup of \(G\) if \(H\) is itself a group under the group operation of \(G\text{.}\) We write \(H\leq G\) to indicate that \(H\) is a subgroup of \(G\text{.}\) A (left) coset of a subgroup \(H\) of \(G\) is a set of the form

\begin{equation*} gH := \{gh \colon h\in H\}. \end{equation*}

The set of all cosets of \(H\) is denoted \(G/H\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} G/H := \{gH\colon g\in G\} \end{equation*}
