Let \(G\) be a group and let \(g\in G\text{.}\) In row \(h\text{,}\) the element \(g\) appears in column \(h^{-1}g\text{.}\) If \(g\) appears in column \(a\) and column \(b\) in row \(h\text{,}\) then we have \(g=ha=hb\text{,}\) so \(a=b\) (by left cancellation). A similar argument shows that \(g\) appears exactly once in each column.
