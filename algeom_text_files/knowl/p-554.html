The three models for elliptic geometry are \((S^2_\Quat,U(\Quat))\text{,}\) \((S^2,\Rot(S^2))\text{,}\) and \((\extC,\S)\text{.}\) The maps

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ccccc} S^2_\Quat \amp \longleftrightarrow \amp S^2 \amp \longrightarrow \amp \extC\\ xi+yj+zk \amp \longleftrightarrow \amp (x,y,z) \amp \stackrel{s}{\longrightarrow} \amp \frac{x+iy}{1-z} \end{array} \end{equation*}

are clearly bijections. The correspondences

\begin{equation*} r \leftrightarrow R_r \leftrightarrow s\circ R_r \circ s^{-1} \end{equation*}

that have been established in the exposition and the exercises establish the necessary conjugacies for elements of \(U(\Quat),\Rot(S^2),\S\text{.}\)
