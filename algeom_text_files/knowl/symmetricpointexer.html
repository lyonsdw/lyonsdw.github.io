Exercise 6. Symmetry with respect to a cline.
  1. Let \(C\) be the unit circle and let \(z\) be real. Show that \(z^{\ast C} = 1/z\text{.}\)Hint
  2. Again, let \(C\) be the unit circle and let \(z\) be any complex number. Use a rotation together with the previous part of this problem to show that \(z^{\ast C} = 1/z^\ast\text{.}\)
  3. Now let \(C\) be a circle of radius \(r>0\) centered at the origin. Use a homothety together with the previous parts of this problem to show that \(z^{\ast C} = r^2/z^\ast\text{.}\)
  4. Let \(C\) be an arbitrary Euclidean circle of radius \(r>0\) and center \(a\in \C\text{.}\) Show that \(z^{\ast C} = \frac{r^2}{(z-a)^\ast}+a\text{.}\)
  5. Let \(C\) be a straight line. Show that \(z^{\ast C}\) is the reflection of \(z\) across \(C\text{.}\)
  6. Show that the definition of symmetric point given here is independent of the choice of the three points \(z_1,z_2,z_3\text{.}\)
