We have

\begin{align*} \text{Area } \amp = \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^\alpha \frac{4\;dr\;d\theta}{(1+r^2)^2} \\ \amp = 2\int_0^{2\pi} \left[ \int_1^{1+\alpha^2} \frac{du}{u}\right] d\theta \;\;(\text{for }u=1+r^2)\\ \amp = 2\int_0^{2\pi}\frac{\alpha^2}{1+\alpha^2}d\theta\\ \amp = \frac{4\pi\alpha^2}{1+\alpha^2}. \end{align*}

In terms of \(r=2\arctan \alpha\text{,}\) we have \(\alpha=\tan\frac{r}{2}\) and

\begin{equation*} \text{Area } = \frac{4\pi\alpha^2}{1+\alpha^2} = \frac{4\pi \tan^2\frac{r}{2}}{\sec^2\frac{r}{2}} = 4\pi \sin^2 \frac{r}{2}. \end{equation*}
