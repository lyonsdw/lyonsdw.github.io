Instructor's solution for Exercise 3.6.3.

By Exercise 3.2.6.8, the tangent direction at \(p\) on the unit circle is \(\pm\beta^\ast\text{.}\) The vector \(\vec{0p}\) is a radius of the unit circle that is orthogonal to the tangent direction, so the tangent direction is given by \(ip\text{.}\) Putting these facts together, we have \(\beta^\ast=\alpha ip\) for some real scalar \(\alpha\text{.}\) Multiplying both sides by \(\beta\) and rearranging yields the desired result.
