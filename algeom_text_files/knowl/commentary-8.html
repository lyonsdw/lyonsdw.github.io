Instructor's solution for Checkpoint 1.2.6.

All three equations can be verified by directly computing the left and right hand sides and comparing. A slightly more elegant approach (assuming you are familiar with the matrix algebra operations of transpose and conjugation) is to first verify that \((M(r^\ast)) = (M(r))^\dagger\text{,}\) where \(\dagger\) denotes the conjugate transpose of a matrix. (1.2.15) follows immediately. Next, verify that \(T(u,v)(T(u,v))^\dagger=(|u|^2+|v|^2)\Id\) (here we are using the same notation used in the solution to Checkpoint 1.2.2). This last matrix corresponds to the quaternion \(|r|^2\text{,}\) so we have established (1.2.17). Finally, use the two equations already established to compute

\begin{equation*} |rs|^2 = (rs)(rs)^\ast = rss^\ast r^\ast = |s|^2 rr^\ast = |r|^2|s|^2, \end{equation*}

then take square roots to establish (1.2.16).
