Exercise 4. The projective linear group action on projective space.

Let \(V\) be a vector space over a field \(\F\) (in this course, the base field \(\F\) is either the real numbers \(\R\) or the complex numbers \(\C\)), and let \(V^\ast := V\setminus\{0\}\) denote the set of nonzero vectors in \(V\text{.}\) The group \(\F^\ast\) of nonzero elements in \(\F\) acts on \(V^\ast\) by scalar multiplication. The set of orbits

\begin{equation*} V^{\ast}/\F^\ast \end{equation*}

is called the projectivization of \(V\text{,}\) or simply projective space, and is denoted \(\Proj(V).\)

  1. Let \(\sim_{\text{proj}}\) denote the equivalence relation that defines the orbits \(V^{\ast}/\F^\ast\) of the group action \(\F^\ast \to \Perm(V^{\ast})\) given by \(\alpha\cdot v=\alpha v\text{.}\) Verify that \(\sim_{\text{proj}}\) is given by \(x\sim_{\text{proj}} y\) if and only if \(x=\alpha y\) for some \(\alpha\in\F^\ast\text{.}\)
  2. Verify that the group of \(GL(V)\) (the group of invertible linear transformations of \(V\)) acts on \(\Proj(V)\) by
    \begin{equation} g\cdot [{v}] = [g({v})]\tag{2.5.1} \end{equation}
    for \(g\in GL(V)\) and \({v}\in V\setminus \{0\}\text{.}\)
  3. Show that the kernel of the map \(GL(V)\to \Perm(\Proj(V))\) given by (2.5.1) is the subgroup \(K=\{\alpha\Id\colon \alpha\in \F^\ast\}\text{.}\)
  4. Conclude that the projective linear group \(PGL(V):=GL(V)/K\) acts on \(\Proj(V)\text{.}\)
  5. Show that \(\F^\ast\) acts on \(GL(V)\) by \(\alpha\cdot T=\alpha T\text{,}\) and that \(PGL(V)\approx GL(V)/\F^\ast\text{.}\)
  6. Show that the map \(\Proj(\R^2)\to S^1\) given by \([(x,y)]\to \frac{(x+iy)^2}{x^2+y^2}\) is well-defined and is a bijection.
  7. Show that the map \(\Proj(\C^2)\to S^2\) given by \([(\alpha,\beta)]\to s^{-1}(\alpha/\beta)\) if \(\beta\neq 0\) and given by \([(\alpha,\beta)]\to (0,0,1)\) if \(\beta=0\) is well-defined and is a bijection.
