Definition 3.1.5. Subgeometry.

We say that a geometry \((X,G)\) is a subgeometry of a geometry \((Y,H)\) if \(X\) is a subset of \(Y\text{,}\) \(G\) is a subgroup of \(H\text{,}\) and the action of \(G\) on \(X\) is compatible with the action of \(m\) on \(Y\) in the sense that \(g\cdot x\) has the same value in both geometries \((X,G)\) and \((Y,H)\) for all \(g\in G, x\in X\text{.}\)
