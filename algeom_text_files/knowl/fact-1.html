Fact 0.2.1. Equivalence relations and partitions.

Let \(X\) be a set. Given an equivalence relation \(\sim\) on \(X\text{,}\) the collection

\begin{equation*} \{[x]\colon x\in X\} \end{equation*}

is a partition of \(X\text{.}\) Conversely, given a partition of \(X\text{,}\) the relation \(\sim\) defined by

\begin{gather*} \text{$x\sim y$ if and only if $x,y$ lie in the same partition set} \end{gather*}

is an equivalence relation.
