Let \(n\geq 1\) be an integer. We write \(GL(n,\R)\) to denote the set of \(n\times n\) invertible matrices with real entries.

Definition 1.1.11. The group of invertible matrices .

The set \(GL(n,\R)\text{,}\) together with the binary operation of matrix multiplication, is called the group of \(n\times n\) invertible matrices, or also the general linear group.
