Let \(G\) be a group, let \(a\) be an element of \(G\text{,}\) and let \(C_a\colon G\to G\) be given by \(C_a(g)=aga^{-1}\text{.}\) The map \(C_a\) is called conjugation by the element \(a\text{.}\)
6. Conjugation by an element is an isomorphism.

Show that \(C_a\) is an isomorphism of \(G\) with itself.

7. Conjugation induces an equivalence relation.

Show that ``is conjugate to'' is an equivalence relation. That is, consider the relation on \(G\) given by \(x\sim y\) if \(y=C_a(x)\) for some \(a\text{.}\) Show that this is an equivalence relation.
