Fact 1.1.5. Symmetries of the regular \(n\)-gon.

The symmetries of the regular \(n\)-gon is the set of all \(R_\theta,F_L\) for the following values of \(\theta,L\text{,}\) where \(O\) is the center of the \(n\)-gon.

\begin{gather} \theta = \frac{k}{n}\text{ revolutions}=k\frac{360}{n} \text{ degrees} = k\frac{2\pi}{n} \text{ radians}, k\in \Z\tag{1.1.1}\\ L=\overline{OP}, \; P \text{ any vertex}\tag{1.1.2}\\ L=\overline{OM}, \; M \text{ the midpoint of any edge}\tag{1.1.3} \end{gather}
This description is redundant. For example, one of the rotational symmetries of the square is by an angle of \(3/4\) revolutions or 270 degrees. This is the same as \(-1/4\) revolutions or \(-90\) degrees. In general, we have \(R_{\frac{k}{n}} = R_{-\frac{n-k}{n}}\text{.}\) There is also redundancy among the mirror symmetries. If \(n\) is even, then each vertex \(P\) has an opposite vertex P' such that \(\overline{OP}=\overline{OP'}\text{,}\) i.e., we have \(F_{\overline{OP}}=F_{\overline{OP'}}\text{.}\) If \(n\) is odd, then each vertex \(P\) has an opposite midpoint M such that \(\overline{OP}=\overline{OM}\text{,}\) i.e., we have \(F_{\overline{OP}}=F_{\overline{OM}}\text{.}\)
