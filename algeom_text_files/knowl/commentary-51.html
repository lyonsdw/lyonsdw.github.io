Instructor's solution for Exercise 2.3.2.1 (proof of Proposition 2.3.4).

It is clear that (i) implies (ii) and (ii) implies (iii). Now suppose (iii) holds. Let \(a\) be an element of \(H\text{.}\) Then \(aa^{-1}=e\) is in \(H\text{,}\) and therefore \(ea^{-1}=a^{-1}\) is in \(H\text{,}\) and furthermore, \(H\) is closed under inversion. Now let \(a,b\) be in \(H\text{.}\) Then \(b^{-1}\) is in \(H\text{,}\) so \(a(b^{-1})^{-1}=ab\) is in \(H\text{.}\) This shows that (i) holds.
