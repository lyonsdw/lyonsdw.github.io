Let \(Tz=\frac{z-p}{1-p^\ast z}\text{,}\) so that \(Tp=0\text{.}\) Let \(a,b\) be the points where \(T(L)\) intersects the unit circle. There is a unique diameter, say \(K\text{,}\) that bisects the Euclidean straight line segment connecting \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\) It is clear that \(K\) is orthogonal to \(T(L)\text{.}\) It follows that \(T^{-1}(K)\) is the unique hyperbolic straight line that passes through \(p\) and is orthogonal to \(L\text{.}\)
