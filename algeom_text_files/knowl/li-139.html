Item a.
Let \(H\) be a nontrivial subgroup of \(\Z\text{,}\) and let \(d\) be the smallest positive element of \(H\) (because of closure under inverses, such a \(d\) is guaranteed to exist). It is clear that \(H\) contains all integer multiples of \(d\text{.}\) Given any element \(a\in H\text{,}\) use the division algorithm to write \(a=dq+r\text{,}\) with \(0\leq r\leq d-1\text{.}\) We see that \(r=a-dq\) is in \(H\text{,}\) so it must be that \(r=0\text{.}\)
