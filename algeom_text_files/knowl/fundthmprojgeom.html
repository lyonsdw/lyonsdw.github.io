Theorem 3.5.2. Fundamental Theorem of Projective Geometry.

Let \(P_0,P_1,P_2,\ldots,P_{n+1}\) be a set of \(n+2\) points in \(\Proj(\F^{n+1})\) such that all subsets of size \(n+1\) are in general position. Let \(Q_0,Q_1,Q_2,\ldots,Q_{n+1}\) be another such set. There exists a unique projective transformation that maps \(P_i\to Q_i\text{,}\) \(0\leq i\leq n+1\text{.}\)
