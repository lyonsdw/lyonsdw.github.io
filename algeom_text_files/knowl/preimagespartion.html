Proposition 0.2.1. Equivalence classes induced by a function.

Let \(f\colon X\to Y\) be a function, and let \(\sim\) be the relation on \(X\) given by \(x\sim y\) if and only if \(f(x)=f(y)\text{.}\) Then \(\sim\) is an equivalence relation. Furthermore, the function \(X/\!\!\sim \; \to f(X)\) given by \([x]\leftrightarrow f(x)\) is a one-to-one correspondence.
