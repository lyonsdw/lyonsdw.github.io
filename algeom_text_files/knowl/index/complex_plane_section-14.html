Using (1.1.10), we have the following for \(r\neq 0\text{.}\)
\begin{equation} \left(r\expi(\theta)\right)\; \left(\frac{1}{r}\expi(-\theta)\right) = 1.\tag{1.1.11} \end{equation}
For \(z=r\expi(\theta)\) with \(r\neq 0\text{,}\) we call \(\frac{1}{r}\expi(-\theta)\) the multiplicative inverse of \(z\text{,}\) denoted \({1}/{z}\) or \(z^{-1}\text{.}\) We define the quotient \(z/w\) or \(\frac{z}{w}\) by
\begin{equation} \frac{z}{w} = zw^{-1}.\tag{1.1.12} \end{equation}
