For the "if" direction, let \(K=\ker(\phi)\) for some homomorphism \(\phi \colon G\to G'\) for some group \(G'\text{.}\) By Item 6 of Proposition 1.4.5, we have a one-to-one correspondence \(G/K\leftrightarrow \phi(G)\text{.}\) The group structure of \(\phi(G)\) (see Item 4) induces the group multiplication (1.4.1) on \(G/K\text{.}\) For the "only if" direction, suppose \(G/K\) is a group under the operation (1.4.1). Let \(\phi\colon G\to G/K\) be given by \(g\to gK\text{,}\) and show that \(K=\ker(\phi)\text{.}\)
