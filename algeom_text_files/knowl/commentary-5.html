We have \(M(1) = \Id\text{,}\) \(M(i)=\twotwo{i}{0}{0}{-i}\text{,}\) \(M(j)=\twotwo{0}{1}{-1}{0}\text{,}\) and \(M(k)=\twotwo{0}{i}{i}{0}\text{.}\) (These are, in order, \(\Id,iZ,iY,iX\text{,}\) where \(X,Y,Z\) are the Pauli matrices.) First, we work out the products of \(M(i),M(j),M(k)\text{.}\) Here is a sample.

\begin{align*} M(i)^2 \amp = \twotwo{i}{0}{0}{-i}\twotwo{i}{0}{0}{-i} = \twotwo{-1}{0}{0}{-1}=-\Id\\ M(i)M(j) \amp = \twotwo{i}{0}{0}{-i}\twotwo{0}{1}{-1}{0} = \twotwo{0}{i}{i}{0}=M(k) \end{align*}

Then we work out all the equations (1.2.9) and (1.2.10). Here is a sample.

\begin{align*} i^2 \amp = Q(M(i)^2) = Q(-\Id) = -1\\ ij\amp =Q(M(i)M(j)) = Q(M(k))=k \end{align*}
