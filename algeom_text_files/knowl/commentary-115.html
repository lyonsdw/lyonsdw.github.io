By definition, Steiner circles of the second kind are sets of the form \(S^{-1}(\{w\colon |w|=r\})\) for some \(w=Sz=\frac{z-p}{z-q}\) (some distinct \(p,q\in C)\text{.}\) Setting \(P=p\text{,}\) \(Q=q\text{,}\) \(k=r\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} S^{-1}(\{|w|=r\}) = \left\{P\colon \frac{d(P,A)}{d(Q,B)}=k\right\}. \end{equation*}
