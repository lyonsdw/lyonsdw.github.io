The component of \(u\) in the \(r\) direction is \((u\cdot r)r\text{,}\) and the desired reflection of \(u\) in the plane normal to \(r\) is \(u-2(u\cdot r)r\text{.}\) The heart of this exercise is to see that \(r\times(u\times r) = u-(u\cdot r)r\text{.}\) This follows immediately from the identity
\begin{equation*} a\times (b\times c) = (a\cdot c)b - (a\cdot b)c, \end{equation*}
which holds for all \(a,b,c\in \R^3\text{.}\) Another way is to use the right-hand rule to see that the vectors on both sides of the equation point in the same direction, and use the formula \(|a\times b|=|a||b|\sin\theta\text{,}\) where \(\theta\) is the angle made by vectors \(a,b\text{,}\) to see that the vectors on both sides have length \(|u|\sin \theta\text{,}\) where \(\theta\) is the angle made by \(u\) and \(r\text{.}\) Now we have
\begin{align*} rur \amp = r(-u\cdot r + u\times r)\;\;(\text{by }\knowl{./knowl/purequatproduct.html}{\text{(1.2.13)}})\\ \amp = r(-u\cdot r) -r\cdot (u\times r) + r\times (u\times r)\;\;(\text{by }\knowl{./knowl/purequatproduct.html}{\text{(1.2.13)}})\\ \amp = u-2(u\cdot r)r \end{align*}
as desired.
