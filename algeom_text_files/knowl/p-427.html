Let \(C\) be a line, and let \(R,T\) be a rotation and a translation, respectively, such that \(RT(C) =\extR\text{.}\) Given a pair \(z,z'\) that are symmetric with respect to \(C\text{,}\) we have that \((RTz)^\ast = RT(z^\ast)\text{,}\) so that \(w=RTz,w'=RT(z^\ast)\) are reflections of one another across the real line. Since \(R,T\) are distance and angle preserving, we conclude that \(z=T^{-1}R^{-1}w,z'=T^{-1}R^{-1}(w')\) must be reflections of one another across the line \(C=T^{-1}R^{-1}(\extR)\text{.}\)
