Instructor's solution for Exercise 2.2.2.9.

By the previous problem Exercise 2.2.2.8, it is enough to show that a cycle can be written as a product of transpositions. Here is a decomposition of a \(k\)-cycle as a product of transpositions.
\begin{equation*} (a_1a_2\ldots a_k) = (a_1a_2)(a_2a_3)\ldots (a_{k-1}a_k) \end{equation*}
