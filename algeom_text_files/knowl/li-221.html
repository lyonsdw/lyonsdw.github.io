Item b.

You just have to check that \(L_{gh}=L_g\circ L_h\text{,}\) \(R_{gh}=R_g\circ R_h\) (here you see why the inverse is needed!), and \(C_{gh}=C_g\circ C_h\text{.}\) Here is the argument for \(L\text{.}\) Similar arguments work for \(R,C\text{.}\) Let \(g,h\in G\text{.}\) We have \(L_g(L_h(x))=g(hx) = (gh)x = L_{gh}(x)\) for all \(x\in G\text{,}\) so we have \(L_g\circ L_h = L_{gh}\text{.}\)
