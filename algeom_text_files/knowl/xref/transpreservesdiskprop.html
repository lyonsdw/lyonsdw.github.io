Proposition 3.3.2. Möbius transformations that map one side of a cline to itself.

Let \(C\) be a cline, and let \(D,E\) be the two disjoint regions of \(\extC \setminus C\text{.}\) Let \(T\) be a Möbius transformation that maps \(D\) onto itself. Then \(T\) also maps \(E\) onto itself, and \(T\) maps \(C\) onto itself.
