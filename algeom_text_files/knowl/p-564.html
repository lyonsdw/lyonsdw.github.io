No, the two geometries are not equivalent. The transformation \((x,y,z)\to (-x,-y,-z)\text{,}\) corresponding to the orthogonal matrix \(-\Id\text{,}\) is an element of the group given in Subsection 3.1.1, but is not conjugate to any element in \(\S\text{.}\) To justify the "not conjugate to an element of \(\S\)" claim, elements of \(\S\) are Möbius transformations, and therefore conformal, and therefore preserve oriented angles. But \(-\Id\) takes the oriented angle \(\angle XZY\) (where we use the shorthand \(X,Y,Z\) for \((1,0,0), (0,1,0), (0,0,1)\text{,}\) respectively), which measures \(+\pi/2\) radians, to the angle \(\angle (-X)(-Z)(-Y)\text{,}\) which measures \(-\pi/2\) radians.
