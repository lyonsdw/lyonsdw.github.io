Item c.
Let \(\phi\colon GL(V)\to \Perm(\Proj(V))\) be the action defined by Equation (2.5.1). It is easy to check that \(K\subseteq \ker(\phi)\text{.}\) Containment in the other direction requires a short linear algebra argument. Suppose \(g\in \ker(\phi)\text{.}\) This means that for every \(v\in V\text{,}\) there is a nonzero scalar \(\lambda_v\) such that \(gv=\lambda_v v\) (that is, every vector in \(V\) is an eigenvector of the operator \(g\)). If \(\dim(V)=1\text{,}\) we are done. If \(\dim(V)\gt 1\text{,}\) choose independent vectors \(v,w\in V\text{.}\) We have \(\lambda_{v+w}(v+w)=g(v+w)=\lambda_v v+\lambda_w w\text{.}\) Because \(v,w\) are independent, it follows that \(\lambda_v=\lambda_w=\lambda_{v+w}\text{.}\) Thus it must be that there is a single \(\lambda\in \F^\ast\) such that \(gv=\lambda v\) for all \(v\in V\text{,}\) that is, \(g\) is an element of \(K\text{.}\)
