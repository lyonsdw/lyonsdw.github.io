In order to obtain a formula for computing \(d_{\extC}(p,q)\text{,}\) we follow the same procedure for hyperbolic distance. First, we find the distance \(d_{\extC}(0,u)\text{,}\) where \(0\leq u\leq 1\text{.}\) Let \(S=(0,0,-1)=s^{-1}(0)\) and let \(U=s^{-1}(u)\text{.}\) Let \(0=(0,0,0)\text{,}\) let \(N=(0,0,1)\text{,}\) let \(\alpha = \angle SNU\) and let \(\theta = \angle SOU\) (see FigureÂ 3.4.20). It is a simple exercise to show that \(\alpha = \theta/2\text{,}\) so that we have

\begin{equation} d_{\extC}(0,u)=d_{S^2}(S,U)=\theta = 2\alpha=2\arctan u. \tag{3.4.9} \end{equation}
