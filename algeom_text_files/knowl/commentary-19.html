We have \((a,b,c)\stackrel{s}{\to} \frac{a+bi}{1-c}\) and \((a,-c,b)\stackrel{s}{\to} \frac{a-ci}{1-b}\) so the question is whether \(\frac{a+bi}{1-c}\stackrel{\twotwo{1}{i}{i}{1}}{\to} \frac{a-ci}{1-b}\text{.}\) The answer is yes, as confirmed by a tedious but straightforward derivation.

\begin{equation*} \frac{a+bi}{1-c}\stackrel{\twotwo{1}{i}{i}{1}}{\to}\frac{\frac{a+bi}{1-c}+i}{i\frac{a+bi}{1-c}+1}= \cdots = \frac{a-ci}{1-b} \end{equation*}
