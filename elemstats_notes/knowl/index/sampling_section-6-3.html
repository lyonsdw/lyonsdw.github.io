By virtue of Equation (2.4.1), it is intuitively reasonable to use ave(observed) to estimate ave(box). It is not so clear, but it turns out to be okay for large enough samples, to use SD(observed) to estimate SD(box). By (2.4.2), it makes sense that our actual ave(observed) will be off from the ave(box) by SE(ave), which we can estimate by
\begin{equation} \text{SE(ave) } \approx \frac{\text{SD(observed)}}{\sqrt{\text{number of draws}}} \;\;\text{(estimated)}\tag{2.4.6} \end{equation}
(times the correction factor, if sampling without replacement). Equation (2.4.6) is called the bootstrap estimate for the SE of the sample average.
