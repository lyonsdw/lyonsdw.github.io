Item 1.a.

Use the facts that the regression line passes close to vertical centers of thin vertical strips, that the regression line passes through the point of averages \((\AVE(X),\AVE(Y))\text{,}\) and has slope equal to \(r\frac{\SD(Y)}{\SD(X)}\text{.}\) Let \((A,B)=(\AVE(X),\AVE(Y))\) be the point of averages and let \((P,Q)\) be the point on the regression line with \(P=24.8\text{,}\) so that \(Q\) is the \(Y\)-coordinate we are looking for. The run is \(P-A=4.67\text{,}\) the rise is \(\text{(slope)(run)}=.82\frac{4.90}{3.65}(4.67)\approx 5.137\text{,}\) and \(Q=\text{rise } + B \approx 21.0\text{.}\)
